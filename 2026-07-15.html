☀️ Big Story of the Day
Canada's own realtor group just quietly admitted it was wrong: 2026 is now a DOWN year for home sales

This is fresh — it dropped a few hours ago. CREA (the Canadian Real Estate Association — the national body that all realtors report through) puts out a forecast a few times a year for where home sales and prices are heading. Back in April they were still calling for a small gain in 2026. This morning they flipped it: they now expect sales to actually fall about 1.4% this year compared to 2025. That's a real U-turn, not a rounding tweak.

On price, they barely moved: the national average is now pegged to rise just 1.1% to about $686,710 for the year — roughly $2,000 lower than what they guessed in spring. In plain English: they're telling you 2026 is a flat, sideways market — not the rebound some headlines keep promising, and not the crash others keep screaming about. Quietly boring, in a way that actually helps a buyer.

Video-worthy because: the group that represents realtors just downgraded its own market — that's the opposite of hype, and it's rare.
