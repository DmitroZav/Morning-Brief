☀️ Big Story of the Day
Must-Know
Iran-US Peace + Gas Prices: What It Means for Your Wallet

The US and Iran signed a peace framework on June 18, ending a brief but brutal conflict that had spiked global oil prices to their highest levels since 2022. Gas at Canadian pumps had jumped 33% year-over-year by May — the main reason Canada's inflation hit 3.2%, the highest since late 2023. With the conflict winding down, relief is on the way, but economists warn it will take months to fully show up in prices.

Why it's video-worthy: every Canadian felt it at the pump. Inflation was the #1 housing affordability killer this year — and now that story is turning. Perfect "things are finally moving in the right direction" angle for your audience.

🧠 Why It Matters (To You)
The Plain-Language Takeaway

Renewing your mortgage this year? About 1.15 million Canadians are expected to renew in 2026. If you locked in at 2% in 2021, you're looking at roughly 4% now — that's hundreds of extra dollars per month. The good news is fixed rates are slowly falling; if you can, a shorter term (1–2 year fixed) might let you catch lower rates sooner.

Thinking about buying? GTA prices are still 4.6% lower than a year ago — you're getting a discount compared to 2025 buyers. But don't sleep on it: the market tightened in May with sales up and new listings down. Inventory is shrinking quietly.

Renting? This is your best rental market in two years. Average asking rent is down almost 8% from the peak. If your lease is up, now is actually a decent time to negotiate or shop around.
