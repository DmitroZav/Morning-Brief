Up to $130,000 Off a New Home — It's Now the Law

Bill C-26 — the Improving Housing Supply Act — received Royal Assent on June 19. The big headline: Ontario buyers of new homes priced up to $1.5 million can now get up to $130,000 back in combined federal and provincial HST (the sales tax on new builds). HST is the combined federal + Ontario sales tax — typically 13% — that gets tacked onto the price of a brand-new home or condo. Until now, this tax made new construction significantly more expensive than resale.

The federal government is also sending $1.7 billion to provinces to cut development fees — extra costs builders pay to cities that ultimately get passed to buyers.

Video-worthy because: This is the single biggest cost reduction for new-home buyers in years. Your audience needs to hear it from someone who lives here and understands the pain of that 13% hit. One catch: final rules aren't locked in yet, and builders are already looking for workarounds — worth flagging honestly.

🧠 Why It Matters (To You)
The Plain-Language Takeaway

If you're a first-time buyer: The HST relief is real and it's big — but only on new builds, and the fine print isn't finalized yet. Don't sign anything based on the headline alone. Ask your agent and lawyer what's confirmed.

If you're a renter: Rents have dropped 19 months in a row. You have more negotiating power than you did two years ago. If you haven't asked your landlord to renegotiate, now might be the moment.

If you're carrying debt: 1 in 7 dollars of the average Canadian household income now goes just to servicing debt — before groceries. Insolvencies hit a 17-year high in April. The rate holds don't help people who are already maxed out.

If you're watching the condo market: The federal government quietly approved a $3.2 billion bailout for condo developers in BC — your tax dollars propping up developers who overbuilt. Ron Butler is loud about this today. Worth watching how it plays out nationally.
