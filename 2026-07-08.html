☀️ Big Story of the Day
Canada is about to cut immigration again — and this time it's aimed straight at housing

The Carney government is moving to tighten immigration further, with new caps on international students and temporary foreign workers, and the stated reason is housing. The logic the government is selling: fewer people arriving each year means less competition for the same short supply of homes and apartments, which is supposed to cool rents and prices. This lands on top of the lower permanent-resident targets already set for 2025–2027.

In plain language: Ottawa has decided that the fastest lever it can actually pull on the housing crisis isn't building more homes (that takes years) — it's slowing how many people come in. For anyone already here, that's a mixed bag. If you rent, less competition could ease the pressure over time. If you're a newcomer still trying to bring family, switch from a study or work permit to permanent residency, or you run a business that hires temporary workers, the door is getting narrower, not wider.
