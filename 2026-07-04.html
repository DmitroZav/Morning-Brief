☀️ Big Story of the Day
The "condo bailout" fight is the story everyone's arguing about this weekend — and it's your tax money

Ottawa and BC are teaming up on a $1.45-billion plan to buy roughly 2,200 empty, unsold condos in Metro Vancouver and turn them into rent-to-own homes. The federal share is about $145 million (10%), BC covers the rest. PM Mark Carney says it's about creating affordable housing. Critics — including opposition leader Pierre Poilievre — call it a developer rescue, a "transfer of wealth from the have-nots to the have-yachts."

Why it's boiling over now: there are about 4,376 finished condos sitting empty in Metro Vancouver, up 76% from a year ago. Developers built them for investors who never showed up. So the question is simple and uncomfortable: should the government spend public money buying units nobody wanted at prices that keep developers whole?

Video-worthy because: This is a proper debate — two reasonable sides, real money, and it touches every renter and taxpayer, not just buyers. Debate = comments. This is your best engagement play of the week if you present both sides honestly and ask viewers where they land.
