🧠 Why It Matters (To You)

The national average crossing $700K looks like a headline win, but the more honest number — the MLS Home Price Index — is still down 4.1% from last year. That means prices are still lower than they were. For a first-time buyer sitting on the sidelines, this is actually still a better entry point than 2024 was, especially with 5-year fixed rates creeping toward 3.99%.

For renters: housing starts dropping 6% means fewer new units are coming to market. Less supply added over the next 18 months = more upward pressure on rents. If you're thinking of buying, the window of softer prices may not stay open much longer.

For newcomers specifically: Canada added nearly 180,000 purpose-built rental units currently under construction — that's a 7%+ boost to the rental stock when they complete. Relief is coming, but slowly.
