☀️ Big Story of the Day
The Bank of Canada just said the words nobody expected: "the economy is growing again"

Yesterday at 9:45 a.m. the Bank of Canada (the country's central bank — it sets the interest rate that everything else follows) held its rate at 2.25% for the sixth time in a row. That part was expected. The surprise was the tone. In his own words, Governor Tiff Macklem said: "After stalling over the past year, economic growth looks to have resumed in Canada." The Bank now thinks the economy grew about 2.5% in the spring quarter — after basically flatlining for a year.

Translation for a normal person: the Bank spent the last year sounding worried about a weak, tariff-hit economy. Yesterday it quietly switched from "hang in there" to "we're turning the corner." It still won't cut rates — because inflation popped to 3.2% on gas prices — but it's no longer in fear mode. It expects inflation to drift back to about 2% by early 2027.

Video-worthy because: for months every headline has been recession, tariffs, doom. The central bank just pushed back on all of it in one sentence. "Even the Bank of Canada says we're growing again" is a calm, surprising, screenshot-worthy line — the opposite of the panic your audience keeps scrolling past.
