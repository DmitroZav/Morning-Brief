☀️ Big Story of the Day
Canada just added jobs and unemployment dropped — great for the economy, awkward for your mortgage

This morning Statistics Canada dropped the June jobs report, and it came in better than expected: employers added 18,000 jobs and the unemployment rate ticked down to 6.5% from 6.6%. Young workers led the way (youth employment +33,000, youth unemployment down to 12.7%), and wages grew a little faster — average hourly pay up 3.3% from a year ago, versus 3.0% the month before.

In plain language: the job market is a bit stronger and people are earning a bit more. That's genuinely good news for anyone with a paycheque. But here's the twist nobody frames for a normal person — a stronger economy gives the Bank of Canada less reason to cut interest rates. The Bank meets next Tuesday, July 15, and markets already put the odds of a rate cut at basically zero (a hold is ~94% priced in, with even a small chance of a hike). Today's numbers just cemented that. There is one asterisk: manufacturing shed 17,000 jobs and is down 61,000 since early 2025 as U.S. tariffs keep biting — so it's not all sunshine.
