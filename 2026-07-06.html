☀️ Big Story of the Day
Canada is building a giant new oil pipeline to the BC coast — to sell to Asia instead of the US

Over the weekend the biggest national story kept growing: Prime Minister Mark Carney, Alberta Premier Danielle Smith, and BC Premier David Eby lined up behind a new "West Coast" oil pipeline — a project estimated at $35–44 billion that would carry about 1 million barrels of oil a day from Alberta, across British Columbia, to the Pacific coast. The point is to ship Canadian oil to Asia instead of depending on the United States as our one big customer.

In plain language: right now Canada sells almost all its oil to the US, often at a discount, because we don't have an easy way to get it anywhere else. A pipeline to the coast means more buyers, better prices, and — the government hopes — more jobs and tax money. The trade-off: BC keeps its northern-coast oil-tanker ban, Ottawa is putting in $10 billion to expand the Port of Vancouver, and environmental groups plus Alberta separatists are already fighting over it from opposite sides.
