🔥 Big Story
Canada's Biggest Trade Deal Dies in 7 Days — Unless Someone Blinks

CUSMA — the Canada-US-Mexico trade agreement that covers $2.7 trillion in trade every year — hits its mandatory review deadline on July 1. Canada formally asked the US to renew it for 16 more years. Trump says he'd rather cancel it entirely. The US trade rep has told Canada its retaliatory tariffs are "a problem" for negotiations.

Why you care: CUSMA is the reason most of the goods you buy are priced the way they are. If it collapses or gets renegotiated on bad terms, expect more tariffs, higher prices on imported goods, job losses in Ontario and Quebec (the most trade-exposed provinces), and a weaker Canadian dollar — all of which feed directly into inflation and make home ownership even harder to reach.

Why it's video-worthy: Every Canadian should know July 1 isn't just Canada Day — it's the deadline on a deal that shapes the price of everything in their life. You can be the first real estate creator to connect the dots between trade talks and people's mortgages.

🎯 The One Thing
Power of sales (bank-forced home sales) are rising sharply in Ontario, according to a real estate lawyer speaking on Ron Butler's channel today. More forced sellers = potential buyer opportunities, but also a warning sign that financial stress is building in the market.

🧠 Why It Matters (To You)

For first-time buyers: The "wait for rates to drop" strategy just got shakier. Yesterday's 3.2% inflation print killed any chance of a July rate cut, and the CUSMA uncertainty is adding economic noise that could push bond yields higher — meaning fixed mortgage rates may edge up before they come down. GTA prices are still 4.6% below last May's peak. That discount window exists now. It may not last through fall.

For renters: If CUSMA gets renegotiated badly or collapses, expect prices on almost everything — food, clothing, appliances, cars — to jump. Ontario and Quebec are most exposed because their economies depend heavily on cross-border manufacturing and trade. More economic pressure means less money left over for rent, savings, or a down payment.

For mortgage holders: Power of sales rising in Ontario is the quiet signal worth watching. Power of sale (also called "foreclosure" in other countries) is when a lender forces a homeowner to sell because they missed too many mortgage payments. More of them showing up means some homeowners are breaking under today's rates. If you're renewing soon, this is a reminder to call a broker now — not when you get the renewal letter.

For newcomers: CUSMA is the deal that makes Canada-US trade work. Most goods in Canadian stores cross the US border at some point. If that deal frays, prices go up and jobs in manufacturing get hit. As newcomers building financial stability here, you're more exposed to this than people with deep savings cushions. Worth following the July 1 deadline closely.
