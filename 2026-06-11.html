☀️ Big Story of the Day
The Bank of Canada held at 2.25% — and admitted it's stuck

Yesterday the Bank of Canada kept its key rate at 2.25% for the fifth straight time. Governor Tiff Macklem's reason, in plain words: the economy is weak (his words: "weak, but not clearly in recession"), which normally means cut rates — but war-driven oil prices are pushing inflation up (CPI 2.8% in April), which normally means raise them. So they did nothing, and Macklem openly said the next move could be a hike OR a cut. Next decision: July 15.

Why it's video-worthy: every variable mortgage and renewal in the country hangs on this — and "even the Bank doesn't know what's next" is a hook normal people instantly feel.

🎯 The One Thing

🧠 Why It Matters (To You)

Mortgage holder renewing in 2026–27: there's a difference nobody explains: renewing (same lender, new term — almost everyone qualifies) vs refinancing (new loan, pull out equity, consolidate debt — you must re-qualify). The BoC says ~10% in Toronto will be locked out of the second option. If your plan was "I'll refinance to pay off the credit cards," check your home's value now, not at renewal.

First-time buyer: prices down 20% from peak sounds like a gift — but the same falling prices are why sellers aren't listing and why some owners can't move. Slow, picky markets reward buyers with financing arranged in advance.

Variable-rate holder / renter: nothing changed yesterday and likely nothing changes July 15. Budget on today's numbers, not on hoped-for cuts.
