☀️ Big Story of the Day
Banks are in a price war over your mortgage — and buyers are winning

For 8 weeks straight, Canada's banks have been undercutting each other on fixed mortgage rates. Mortgage broker Ron Butler called it out this morning: two major banks keep slashing rates below the rest, forcing everyone else to follow. This is not normal — banks almost never compete this openly on mortgages.

Meanwhile, CREA released May sales data today showing the national average home price hit $702,079 — the first time it's cracked $700K in nearly two years. Sales jumped 5.5% from April to May. The spring market is finally showing up, just a month late.

Why it's video-worthy: "banks fighting over your mortgage" is a news hook that actually helps people. It's rare, it's happening now, and most viewers have no idea it's going on.

🎯 The One Thing
National home sales jumped 5.5% in May and the average price is back above $700K for the first time in 23 months — while banks are openly competing on fixed rates. If you've been sitting on the fence, the market just gave you two reasons to pay attention.

🧠 Why It Matters (To You)

If you're a first-time buyer: the bank rate war is genuinely good news — it means shopping around for your mortgage right now could save you thousands. Don't just take the rate your bank offers. Get 3 quotes.

If you're a renter watching the market: prices are still down year-over-year in Toronto, but the month-over-month momentum is shifting. The window where buyers have leverage may be narrowing.

If you're a newcomer: the national average is $702K but Toronto is $1.07M. The city is expensive, but outside the GTA — Hamilton, Kitchener, London — you can still find homes well below the national average. Geography is everything in Canada right now.
