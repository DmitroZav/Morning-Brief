☀️ Big Story of the Day
Canada Day 2026 — and it's a big one

Today is Canada's 159th birthday. But this year is different: Canada is co-hosting the FIFA World Cup this summer, with games happening right here in Toronto and Vancouver. That's the whole world watching us while we figure out if people can still afford to live here.

Meanwhile, in a quieter piece of news dropped yesterday, the government is actively reviewing the foreign buyer ban (which expires January 2027) — with a likely pivot to the Australia model: foreigners can buy new builds but not existing homes. That's a potentially big shift for the condo market, and it's worth knowing about.

Video-worthy because: Canada Day = emotions are high, reach is up, and the contrast of "celebrating Canada while housing is still broken" is exactly the kind of honest tension your audience connects with.

🧠 Why It Matters (To You)

If you're renting right now, rents are actually going down — the first sustained drop in years. If you're trying to buy in the GTA, prices are still 4–5% below last year, fixed rates have edged down, and the BoC isn't expected to raise rates. That combination hasn't existed in a long time.

The foreign buyer ban review is the wildcard. If Canada follows Australia and opens new construction to foreign buyers in 2027, pre-construction condos and new builds could see a surge in demand. If you're sitting on the fence about a pre-con purchase, this is worth knowing.

And one number that should shake everyone: 30,092 people left Canada in Q1 alone — the biggest Q1 emigration on record. Housing cost is the #1 reason cited. This isn't just a policy debate. It's real people giving up and leaving.
