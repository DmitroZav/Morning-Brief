☀️ Big Story of the Day
Over a million Canadians renew their mortgage this year — and a lot of them are about to get a nasty surprise

Here's the story quietly building under all the condo-crash headlines: about 1.15 million Canadian households have a mortgage coming up for renewal in 2026, with another 940,000 lined up for 2027. Renewal just means your mortgage term ended and you have to sign a new one at today's rate — you don't get to keep your old rate forever. And a huge share of these people locked in dirt-cheap 5-year fixed rates back in 2020 and 2021, when money was almost free.

In plain language: someone who signed at roughly 2% is now renewing into something closer to 4%. The Bank of Canada's own math says the typical fixed-rate borrower renewing this year is looking at a payment jump in the range of 15–20%, and about 1 in 10 of the people renewing in 2026 will see their payment rise more than 40%. Real-world example floating around this weekend: a family that bought an average home in 2020 could be paying roughly $400–$600 more a month starting this summer — thousands more a year, for the exact same house.

Video-worthy because: everyone's making the scary "condo meltdown" reel. Almost nobody is talking to the million-plus regular homeowners who are about to feel this in their own bank account — and telling them what they can actually do about it before they sign.
