Inflation Just Jumped to 3.2% — Released This Morning

Statistics Canada released May's inflation numbers this morning: prices are rising at 3.2% per year, up from 2.8% in April. The main culprit is gasoline — up a stunning 33% compared to last May, driven by ongoing conflict in the Middle East cutting global oil supply. 🧠 Why It Matters (To You)

For first-time buyers: Today's inflation number is frustrating — it means the BoC almost certainly won't cut rates at the July 15 meeting. The mortgage rates you're seeing today (~4%) are probably the rates for the rest of summer. If you've been waiting for rates to drop before buying, that plan just got pushed back. On the flip side: GTA prices are still 4.6% cheaper than last May — that discount won't last forever either.

For renters: Gas up 33% and groceries up 4.3% is money directly out of your pocket every month. Less cash left over for rent means more financial stress. Advertised rents across Canada did fall to a 33-month low earlier this year as immigration slowed — but with inflation rising, landlords will feel pressure to push rents up again at renewal.

For mortgage holders renewing soon: Today's inflation surprise means don't count on rate drops before your renewal. Lock in a conversation with a mortgage broker now. Fixed rates near 4% are historically reasonable — better to renew with clarity than gamble on a cut that may not come until 2027.

For newcomers: When gasoline goes up 33%, everything gets more expensive — delivery, groceries, services. The weak Canadian dollar (70.5 cents US) amplifies this since Canada imports a lot of goods. This is the real cost-of-living squeeze that doesn't show up in the headline number.
